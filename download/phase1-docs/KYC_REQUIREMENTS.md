# KYC (Know Your Customer) Requirements

## 1. KYC Overview

The platform implements a progressive Know Your Customer (KYC) verification system designed to comply with international Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and Counter-Financing of Terrorism (CFT) standards. KYC verification is a mandatory prerequisite for accessing real fund deposit and withdrawal capabilities on the platform. Users can browse investment plans and interact with the demo mode without KYC, but any real financial activity requires at least Level 1 (Basic) verification. The progressive verification model allows users to start with minimal documentation and unlock higher deposit limits and investment plan tiers by providing additional verification at each level. This approach balances regulatory compliance requirements with user onboarding friction, enabling users to begin investing quickly with basic verification while reserving full platform access for those who complete enhanced due diligence. The KYC system integrates with Cloudinary for secure document storage, uses automated checks for document validity and manipulation detection, and maintains a complete audit trail of all verification decisions.

## 2. Verification Levels

### Level 0 (Unverified)

Level 0 is the default state for all newly registered accounts and requires only email verification to reach. Users at Level 0 can browse all publicly available investment plan information, access the demo mode to practice investing with simulated funds, and manage basic account settings. However, Level 0 users are completely blocked from depositing real funds, activating investment plans with real money, initiating withdrawals, or participating in the referral commission program. This level serves as the initial onboarding gate, ensuring that every user who proceeds to financial activity has a confirmed, reachable email address on file. The transition from Level 0 to Level 1 requires the user to initiate a KYC verification request and submit the required documentation. Users can remain at Level 0 indefinitely if they only wish to use the demo mode.

### Level 1 (Basic)

Level 1 verification requires the upload and successful verification of a single government-issued photo identification document. Once verified, users gain the ability to deposit real funds up to a cumulative limit of $2,000 and can invest exclusively in the Basic plan tier ($200–$4,999, 24-hour duration). Withdrawal capability is activated with a daily limit of $500. Level 1 is designed for users making smaller investments who want to get started quickly with minimal documentation friction. The ID verification at this level confirms the user's identity through document authenticity checks and basic biometric matching between the document photo and any available profile information. Users at Level 1 who attempt to deposit or invest beyond their limits will be prompted to upgrade to Level 2.

### Level 2 (Standard)

Level 2 verification requires both a government-issued ID (same as Level 1) and a proof of address document. The proof of address must be a recent utility bill, bank statement, or government tax document that clearly shows the user's full name and residential address. Once verified, users can deposit up to $10,000 in cumulative real funds and gain access to both the Basic and Silver plan tiers ($5,000–$9,999, 72-hour duration). The daily withdrawal limit increases to $5,000. Level 2 verification adds an address confirmation layer on top of identity verification, reducing the risk of fraudulent accounts using stolen identities. This level is suitable for mid-tier investors who are committing more significant capital to the platform. Users must successfully complete Level 1 requirements before submitting Level 2 documentation.

### Level 3 (Premium)

Level 3 is the highest verification level and requires three forms of verification: a government-issued ID, proof of address, and a live selfie taken while holding the ID document beside their face. This three-factor verification provides the strongest assurance of identity authenticity and is required for users investing at the highest tiers. Level 3 users can deposit up to $100,000 and have access to all four investment plan tiers including Gold ($10,000–$49,999, 7 days) and Platinum ($50,000–$100,000, 14 days). The daily withdrawal limit is set at $50,000, accommodating larger investors who may need to move significant funds. The selfie verification at Level 3 adds a real-time biometric component that makes it substantially more difficult for bad actors to use stolen or forged documents. Users must complete Levels 1 and 2 before submitting Level 3 documentation, though all three levels can be submitted together in a single session if the user has all documents ready.

## 3. Accepted Documents

### ID Documents

The platform accepts three types of government-issued photo identification: a valid passport, a national identity card, or a driver's license. All submitted ID documents must be currently valid with a visible expiration date that has not passed. The document must include a clear, unobstructed photograph of the document holder, their full legal name, date of birth, and the document's unique identification number. Passports are accepted from all countries recognized by the platform's compliance framework. National ID cards and driver's licenses are accepted from a defined list of jurisdictions; documents from sanctioned countries or territories are automatically rejected. The photograph on the document must be of sufficient quality for automated biometric matching, meaning it should not be blurred, overexposed, or obscured by glare. Both the front and back of ID cards and driver's licenses must be uploaded, while passports require only the photo page.

### Proof of Address

Proof of address documents must be issued by a recognized institution and must clearly display the user's full name and current residential address matching the information provided in their profile. Accepted documents include utility bills (electricity, gas, water, internet), bank statements, and government-issued tax documents or official correspondence. The document must be dated within the last three months from the date of submission; older documents will be automatically rejected during the initial screening. Electronic statements and online-generated bills are accepted as long as they display the issuing institution's name or logo and are not visibly altered. Screenshots of online portals are accepted only if the full document is captured, including any headers, footers, and the institution's branding. The address on the document must match the address the user has provided in their profile; mismatches will result in rejection with instructions to update their profile or submit a different document.

### Selfie Verification

The selfie verification is required exclusively for Level 3 (Premium) verification. The user must take a photograph of themselves holding their submitted ID document beside their face, ensuring that both their face and the ID document are clearly visible in the same frame. The photo must be well-lit with no significant shadows or glare on the face or the document. The user's face must be unobstructed, without sunglasses, hats, or face coverings that would prevent biometric matching. The ID document in the selfie must be the same document submitted for the ID verification step, and the text on the document must be legible in the photograph. This requirement ensures that the person submitting the KYC is the same person depicted on the identification document, providing a strong deterrent against identity theft and fraudulent verification attempts.

## 4. Upload Requirements

All KYC documents must be uploaded in one of three supported file formats: JPEG (.jpg or .jpeg), PNG (.png), or PDF (.pdf). Each individual document file must not exceed 5 megabytes in size; files exceeding this limit will be rejected at the upload stage with a clear error message directing the user to compress or rescan the document. For image-based uploads (JPEG and PNG), the document must be captured at a minimum resolution of 300 DPI to ensure that all text and details are legible for manual review and any automated processing. Scanned documents are preferred over photographs taken with a mobile device, though high-quality mobile photographs are accepted if they meet the clarity and resolution requirements. For ID cards and driver's licenses, both the front and back must be uploaded as separate files, even if the back contains only machine-readable zones or minimal information. PDF documents must contain exactly one page per file; multi-page PDFs should be split into individual files before upload. The upload interface should provide clear visual guides and previews to help users confirm their documents meet quality standards before submission.

## 5. Verification Process

The KYC verification workflow follows a structured pipeline from document submission through final decision. The user initiates the process by navigating to the verification section of their profile, selecting their target verification level, and uploading the required documents through a guided step-by-step interface. Upon submission, all documents are immediately uploaded to Cloudinary's private folder with restricted access, ensuring that documents are not publicly accessible at any point. The system performs automated pre-screening checks including file format validation, file size enforcement, basic image quality assessment, and extraction of visible text for record-keeping. The submission then enters the KYC Officer review queue, where it appears in the order received. A KYC Officer opens the submission, reviews each document against the verification criteria, checks for signs of tampering or forgery, and verifies that the documents are consistent with each other (name matches across all documents, address matches between ID and proof of address, selfie matches the ID photo). The Officer then approves or rejects the submission. Approvals immediately upgrade the user's verification level and grant access to the corresponding deposit limits and plan tiers. Rejections include a mandatory written reason explaining why the documents did not meet requirements, and the user is notified via in-app notification and email. Rejected users can re-upload corrected documents and resubmit without penalty, though the system tracks the total number of submission attempts for compliance monitoring.

## 6. Verification Timeframes

The platform commits to specific service level agreements for KYC verification turnaround times to ensure users can begin investing without unnecessary delays. Level 1 (Basic) verifications, which involve a single ID document review, are targeted for completion within 24 hours of submission during business days. Level 2 (Standard) verifications, which require review of both an ID and a proof of address document, are targeted for completion within 48 hours. Level 3 (Premium) verifications, which involve three documents including a selfie that may require enhanced biometric comparison, are targeted for completion within 72 hours. These timeframes represent targets under normal operating conditions and assume that the submission queue is not experiencing unusual volume. During periods of high registration activity, the platform may extend these targets and will communicate any delays to affected users via in-app notification. Business hours are defined as 24/7 for automated processing and 8:00 AM to 10:00 PM UTC for manual KYC Officer review. Submissions received outside of business hours begin their processing window at the start of the next business period.

## 7. Re-verification

Periodic re-verification is required to maintain the accuracy and currency of user identity information on the platform. All users with Level 1 or higher verification must complete re-verification every 12 months from their last successful verification date. The re-verification process mirrors the original verification process for the user's current level, requiring fresh submission of the same document types. Users receive advance notification at 30 days, 14 days, and 7 days before their re-verification deadline via in-app notification and email. If a user fails to complete re-verification by the deadline, their account is automatically restricted to Level 0 capabilities: they can no longer initiate new deposits or investments, though existing active investments continue to maturity and withdrawals of matured returns remain accessible. The restriction is lifted immediately upon successful re-verification. In addition to periodic re-verification, the system can trigger ad-hoc re-verification requests based on specific risk indicators, including a change in the user's registered country or address, detection of login patterns from new high-risk geographies, or flags generated by the compliance monitoring system. The platform also tracks the expiration dates of submitted ID documents and initiates re-verification proactively when a document is approaching its expiry date, typically starting the notification process 60 days before expiration.

## 8. Gift Card Verification

Gift card deposits follow a separate but related verification workflow that is distinct from the standard KYC process. When a user selects the gift card payment method, they are prompted to upload a clear screenshot or photograph of the physical or digital gift card showing the card brand, denomination value, card number, and any applicable PIN or security code. The uploaded image is stored securely in Cloudinary and routed to the Admin review queue specifically designated for gift card verification. The reviewing Admin verifies the card's validity by checking that the brand is among the platform's accepted gift card providers, the displayed value matches the user's stated deposit amount, and there are no visible signs that the card has been previously used or redeemed. Admins may cross-reference card details against known fraud databases and check for patterns suggesting stolen or fraudulently obtained gift cards. Upon successful verification, the equivalent USD value is credited to the user's wallet, and the user is notified via in-app notification and email confirmation. If the gift card is rejected, the Admin provides a specific reason (invalid brand, suspected fraud, unreadable card details, etc.) and the user can submit a different gift card. The target turnaround time for gift card verification is 1 to 4 hours during business hours. Gift card deposits are available to users at Level 1 and above, consistent with the general requirement that real fund deposits require at least basic KYC verification.

## 9. Data Protection

KYC documents contain highly sensitive personal information and are protected by multiple layers of security. All documents are encrypted at rest using AES-256 encryption both within Cloudinary's storage infrastructure and during transit via TLS 1.3. Access to KYC documents is strictly limited to KYC Officers (for active review assignments) and Super Admins; even Admins without KYC Officer duties cannot access document files. Every access to a KYC document is logged with the accessor's identity, timestamp, the specific document accessed, and the associated user record, creating a complete access audit trail. The platform retains KYC documents for a period of 5 years from the date of the last verification action, aligned with international AML regulatory retention requirements. After the retention period expires, documents are permanently and irreversibly deleted from Cloudinary storage, with the deletion event recorded in the audit log. Users exercising their GDPR right of access can request a copy of all KYC data held about them, and the platform must fulfill such requests within 30 days. Users exercising their right to erasure can request deletion of their KYC documents, though the platform may retain certain records if required by applicable AML regulations, in which case the user is informed of the specific regulatory basis for retention. The system includes automatic access pattern monitoring to detect any unauthorized or unusual access to KYC documents, with alerts generated for investigation.

## 10. Compliance Flags

The KYC system implements automated compliance flagging to identify potentially high-risk users and transactions in accordance with international sanctions and anti-money laundering frameworks. All user registrations and KYC submissions are automatically screened against major sanctions lists including the U.S. Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) Specially Designated Nationals list, the European Union consolidated sanctions list, and the United Nations Security Council sanctions list. A match or near-match against any sanctions list triggers an immediate hold on the user's account and escalates the case to Super Admin review before any verification can proceed. Users registering from or providing addresses in high-risk countries, as defined by the Financial Action Task Force (FATF) grey and black lists, are automatically flagged for enhanced due diligence and may be subject to additional verification requirements or restricted to demo mode only. The system tracks the number of failed verification attempts per user and flags accounts that exceed three failed submissions for manual review, as repeated failures may indicate an attempt to find gaps in the verification process. Document manipulation detection runs automated analysis on uploaded images to identify signs of digital alteration, splicing, or forgery, including checks for inconsistent pixel patterns, metadata manipulation, and text irregularities. Any flagged submission is routed to a senior KYC Officer for enhanced review, and the flag is recorded in the user's compliance history for ongoing monitoring purposes.
